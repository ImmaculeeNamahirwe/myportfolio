Leadership reflection Essay

Firstly, I am Immaculee Namahirwe, a student at African Leadership University currently pursuing a Bachelor of Entrepreneurial Leadership. I am deeply passionate about wildlife conservation by introducing cutting-edge technology in that field and supporting actions that mitigate climate change in Rwanda, such as sustainable agricultural practices.

My path as an entrepreneurial leader has been shaped by an abundance of life-changing events. The establishment of the FROMGREEN Rwanda, which was motivated by a strong desire to address food insecurity and climate change, was one significant event. I learned from this experience how crucial it is to combine technical expertise with community involvement in order to make a significant and long-lasting impact. For example, developing grafted avocado tree nurseries was a chance to empower communities with sustainable farming methods in addition to being a technical skill.

Being a part of the FXB Climate Advocacy Program was another life-changing event. I applied my strong foundation in climate policies and advocacy tactics from the program to FROMGREEN's initiatives. Through effective stakeholder communication, I was able to build partnerships that increased our influence. My faith in the effectiveness of information exchange and group effort was strengthened by these encounters.

I have used a variety of leadership theories that have guided my decisions and actions along the way. For instance, when I worked with communities in Musanze District, I adopted the idea of servant leadership. I made sure our projects were sustainable and inclusive by putting their needs first and including them in decision-making. To encourage my team at FROMGREEN Rwanda to embrace creative ideas, like establishing tree nurseries with a target of two million trees by 2027, I also used transformational leadership.

When I think back on my educational journey, I see how much my knowledge, abilities, and perspective have changed. African Leadership University's theoretical education and real-world experiences, like my internship at Natural State, have greatly influenced how I approach problem-solving. I've become a better leader as a result of my increased comprehension of sustainability, project management, and community involvement.

The capacity to close the gap between theory and practice has been one of the most important results of my educational journey. For instance, I had to apply financial management theories to actual situations while working on a business budget template for FROMGREEN. In addition to improving my technical proficiency, this experience reaffirmed the significance of financial accountability and transparency.

My mission to empower communities and fight climate change is closely aligned with the insights I gained from my learning journey. The FROMGREEN project report and budget template, among other artifacts I worked on, are concrete examples of my dedication to developing sustainable solutions. My plans for after graduation have also been influenced by these experiences, and one of them is to study public policy at any Post-graduate University I will get,to become an expert in climate policies.

To sum up, my path has been one of constant development motivated by a desire to change the world. Combining educational opportunities, leadership theories, and real-world applications has equipped me to take on new challenges and help create a more sustainable, greener future.
